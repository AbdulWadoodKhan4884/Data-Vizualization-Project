Project: Tactical Biomechanics and Global Performance Mapping in Professional Tennis

Overview
- Theme: Tennis performance (serve efficiency, lefty advantage, geographic talent distribution).
- Narrative arc: from biomechanical insight (surface effects) → tactical nuance (lefty advantage under pressure) → global distribution (where talent emerges).

Storyboard (scrollytelling beats)

1. Intro / Hook
- Visual: Hero banner with title, short subtitle and a dynamic court graphic.
- Text: One-sentence research question and motivation.
- Interaction: Reader can pick a player from a small search box (optional).

2. Beat 1 — The Biomechanics of Surface Dominance (Q1)
- Visual: Violin/box plot of First Serve Efficiency by surface (Clay / Grass / Hard).
- Author-driven: Scroll text highlights summary insight (Grass highest median, Clay lowest).
- Reader-driven: Toggle to restrict to Top-20 players or individual players to compare.
- Annotation: Callouts pointing to median/variance differences.

3. Beat 2 — The Lefty Advantage (Q2)
- Visual: Faceted bar or small-multiples showing Break Point Save % vs Opponent Hand across surfaces.
- Author-driven: Step-by-step text explains the clutch-moment effect.
- Reader-driven: Filter by surface and surface + player handedness; click a bar to reveal match examples.
- Micro-animation: Bars animate in when active; tooltip explains statistical significance.

4. Beat 3 — Geographical Evolution of Talent (Q3)
- Visual: Choropleth world map of player counts / winners with a time slider (1990s → 2020s).
- Author-driven: Narrative highlights Eastward shift and deconcentration from USA.
- Reader-driven: Time slider and hover for country drilldown; clicking a country lists top players.

5. Finale — Synthesis & Takeaways
- Visual: Compact dashboard summarizing three main takeaways with share/export buttons.
- Text: Recommendations for coaches/analysts and links to data & code.

6. Credits & Acknowledgements
- Team names, student IDs, dataset sources, libraries used.
